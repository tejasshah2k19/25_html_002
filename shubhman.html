Shubman Gill (born 8 September 1999) is an Indian international cricketer who plays for the India national team in all formats. Gill captains the Test team, vice-captains the ODI side and has captained the T20I team.[1] He is nicknamed the Prince of Indian cricket.[2] Gill in domestic cricket, represents Punjab and captains the Gujarat Titans in the Indian Premier League. A right-handed opening batter, Gill made his international debut for India in January 2019 against New Zealand.[3]

In the ODI cricket, he is the fastest player to reach 2000 runs in 38 innings and 2500 runs in 50 innings. [4] He also holds the record for the youngest cricketer to score a double century at the age of 23 in ODI as well .[5] With his country, he won the 2025 ICC Champions Trophy as vice captain.
